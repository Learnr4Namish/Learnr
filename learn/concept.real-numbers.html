Addition of whole numbers in number line

Adding numbers in a number line is quiet easy, isn't it? To add a number in a number line, all you need is to move to the right side of the number line. However, this addimg can't be explained but can be understood by you by seeing examples.

Ex 1. Perform 3 + 5 in a number line.

What is being done is that the first number is always our starting number, ex 3 in this case. Now the last or the second number represents the steps we need to move in the number line, ex 5 in this case. So, we actually chose 3 as our starting points, move 5 steps to the right using curved lines. After reachimg 5 steps forward, the number on which the line stops is our final answer, like 8 in this example. Thus 3 + 5 is 8. But what about questions that have three numbers to add, like 3 + 5 + 7, nothing difficult, though there lies a difference between the two, the second one is also easy to do. Let's do that in our next example.

Ex 2. Perform 3 + 5 + 7 in a number line.

Do you see how we have performed addition of Whole Numbers in our number line? Yes, it is as easy as that, all you need to do is to start from 3, move 5 steps and after you reach 8, then again move 5 steps further and we stop at 15, so you final answer is 15.

It is also easy to perform subtraction of whole numbers in number line. Let's take one example, say we take 5 - 3 and now solve that in a number line.

Ex 3. Perform 5 - 3 in a number line.

What is actually being done is that our starting point was chosen as 5, the first number. And there you look, there is a minus, (-) sign. Unlike addition, we will have to move backwards instead of going forward. So, we start from 5, move 3 steps backwards and the final point where we stop is our answer. So our answer is '3'. Just like addition, if thre are two or more numbers given to be subtracted, you have to subtract again and again on the number line. Now is the time to learn about so called 'Rational Numbers'.

Rational Numbers.

A rational number is a number which is written in the form of p/q, where p and q are integers and q ≠ 0. What does that mean? This means that rational numbers are written as numerators thaat can be any integer but the denominator should alays be a non-zero integer, or we may get a value which is actually not-defined, meaning which doesn't have any value or which isn't defined in this very existing world. Have you noticed something else? Have you noticed the world 'Rational'? Rational means that any number that terminates after a specific period of time or we can also call them as terminating numbers because there are certain numbers like 'pi' which is a non-terminating or repeating number having a value of 3.14159265 and so on and this also means that no matter even if you spend your whole lifetime finding the value of 'pi', you just can't! These numbers are called irrational numbers which you are going to learn very soon. Now what we will be doing is that we will be knowing about irrational numbers. They are just the opposite of rational numbers.

Irrational Numbers.

An irrational number is a number which can't be written in the form of p/q where p and q are integers and q ≠ 0. This means that numbers like √2 or π are all irrational numbers as said before. No matter even if you die or your son's son's grandson dies, none of you will ever be able to find the value of this interesting irrational number. We have learn't a lot about real numbers now!
