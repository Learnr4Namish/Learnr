A body at rest will always be at rest and a body in motion will always continue it's uniform state of motion unless an external force is not applied on it. Simple Pendulum

Can you see the image above, it's the image of a simple pendulum. A simple pendulum is a mass hanged using a wire with a strong holding capacity and frictionless surface. Are you getting confused? There is nothing to get confused. Definitions don't always give the clear picture, do they? We need to get deeper and see what's inside. Pendulum follows the periodic motion. It get hanged up with the help of a wire or a String. The heavy mass suspended through the string is known as the 'bob'. The bob may be heavy or light, but the main point is what I just wrote on the definition. The coming back of bob to it's original position after it's suspension is known as Oscillation. If it has come to it's original position 4 times, then the pendulum is said to have covered 4 oscillations. Don't understand what I just said? Well, Suppose I have a simple pendulum in my hand and I release it. It goes on it's way and comes again to it's original position. So this means that my pendulum has covered one oscillation. Now, I haven't applied any force, that is pull or push. It goes again on it's way and comes back again to it's original position. Now, my pendulum has covered 2 oscillations. Now if this happens 2 more times, then my pendulum will have covered 4 oscillations in total. This is what I wanted to tell you. The time taken by the pendulum to cover one oscillation is known as the 'Time Period'. For example say I take one pendulum and I suspend it, It covered one oscillation and It took 4 second to come back to it's original position. So, in this case, 4 seconds is the time period for my pendulum. There are certain things that affect the time period of a simple pendulum. Let's have a look at some of them:-

  • The height at which the bob was pulled
  • The length of the string of the pendulum

If you pull the bob of a pendulum to a certain height, then you actually mean that you have applied a certain force. The more the force, the more is the force and more is the time period. You are actually increasing the time period of that pendulum when you are pulling the bob to a higher height. But the question arises, Why? Since you pull the bob to a height, the bob has to cover more distance and that's why the time period increases. But a pendulum's time period doesn't depend on this thing. It also depends on the length of the string. Let's see.
